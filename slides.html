Learning Objectives

After this workshop, you should be able to:

  • Identify appropriate experimental techniques that can be applied to solving specific biological questions

  • Design experiments including the selection of appropriate controls

Consider how the tools at your disposal can be applied to answering biological questions

  • Use appropriate techniques to study a problem/question of interest (understand the technique + its limitations)

  • Include appropriate controls

  • Biological context, environmental conditions, & experimental set-up matter

What are the tools at your disposal?

  • Your brain (think critically)

  • The scientific literature (what is already known)

  • Toolkit: known and already developed techniques

  • Data analysis techniques (how are you going to analyse & interpret your results?)

As an illustration, we will work through an example:

We have a novel strain of Escherichia coli that appears to be quite virulent

Its genome encodes a protein predicted to be a novel transcription factor, VirA

Hypothesis: VirA regulates virulence gene expression in this E. coli strain

We have a novel strain of Escherichia coli that appears to be quite virulent

Its genome encodes a protein predicted to be a novel transcription factor, VirA

Hypothesis: VirA regulates virulence gene expression in this E. coli strain

How can we test this hypothesis? What experiment(s) would you like to do? 1

  1. assuming unlimited resources and funding

Genetics approach – how would you create mutant strain(s) for analysing the role of VirA in your strain? (deletion, overexpression, other mutants?)

  • What method would you use?
  • What controls are necessary?
  • What are the advantages/limitations of this approach?

Caveats/limitations mostly centre around possibility of inadvertently introducing off-target mutations/suppressor mutations/mutations that have polar effects on downstream genes, etc.

Controls: Complement mutants, include empty vector controls (when complementing or over-expressing); keep in mind the usual caveats for gene dosage, position, the usual concerns.

Biochemistry approach - how would you isolate and purify the VirA protein if you wanted to study it in vitro?

  • What method would you use?
  • What controls are necessary?
  • What are the advantages/limitations of this approach?

Caveats/limitations for biochemical approaches mostly centre around the fact that proteins do not necessarily behave the same way in vitro as they do in vivo

Specific concerns about purified proteins: did you have to tag it to purify, does the tag affect structure/function, is the protein identical to the protein in vivo (are you missing any PTMs, interacting partners, etc.)

  • What method(s) would you use to study virA mutant phenotypes?
  • What controls are necessary?
  • Can you predict what the results of this experiment might look like, and how would you analyse & interpret the data?
  • What are the advantages/limitations of this approach?
  • For any experiments involving animals, what is required in terms of ethics?

Can discuss infection assays and model systems here, but plenty of other potentially interesting phenotypes to discuss here, e.g.:

  • biofilm formation assay
  • effector protein secretion/virulence protein production
  • motility, quorum sensing, other virulence-related phenotypes

Choose another experiment

What DNA sequences does VirA bind?

  • What method would you use to determine what DNA sequences VirA binds to?
  • What controls are necessary?
  • Can you predict what the results of this experiment might look like, and how would you analyse & interpret the data?
  • What are the advantages/limitations of this approach?

Methods here could include:

  • EMSA
  • ChIP-seq or ChIP-exo
  • DNA footprinting

Consider the advantages/limitations of each (e.g., does in vitro binding relate to what happens in vivo?)

Consider, does DNA binding involve a small molecule or contact with other proteins? How would you go about experimentally identifying a VirA ligand?

Choose another experiment

What gene(s) does VirA regulate?

  • What method would you use to identify the VirA regulon?
  • What controls are necessary?
  • Can you predict what the results of this experiment might look like, and how would you analyse & interpret the data?
  • What are the advantages/limitations of this approach?

Methods here might include:

  • RNA-seq, microarray, or other way of assaying levels of potential target genes, e.g. in wild-type vs virA mutant strains (remember to complement the mutant strain)
  • bioinformatic approaches to identify candidate VirA binding sites (based on previous knowledge of where it binds)
  • If defining a regulon, remember caveats such as the effects of medium and environmental conditions on what is actually being transcribed in the cell; e.g., if assaying in LB, might not observe much if VirA regulon not normally expressed under those conditions (remember, for in vivo experiments with animals - ethics!)

Choose another experiment

When and to what extent is VirA expressed in the cell?

  • What method would you use to measure expression of VirA?
  • What controls are necessary?
  • Can you predict what the results of this experiment might look like, and how would you analyse & interpret the data?
  • What are the advantages/limitations of this approach?

Methods here might include:

  • looking at mRNA levels (Northern, q-RT-PCR, microarray, RNA-seq)
  • looking at protein levels (Western blot or similar)
  • using reporter genes (transcriptional or translational)
  • If looking at expression remember caveats such as the effects of medium and environmental conditions; e.g., if assaying in LB, might not observe much if VirA not normally expressed under those conditions (remember, for in vivo experiments with animals - ethics!)

What protein(s) does VirA interact with?

  • How would you identify/validate potential VirA-interacting proteins?
  • What controls are necessary?
  • Can you predict what the results of this experiment might look like, and how would you analyse & interpret the data?
  • What are the advantages/limitations of this approach?

Methods here might include:

  • co-IP, BACTH, various biophysical assays…
  • keep in mind that each has limitations & would normally expect to see multiple lines of evidence to confirm protein-protein interactions…

Choose another experiment

What regulates VirA?

  • How would you identify/validate potential regulators of VirA?
  • What controls are necessary?
  • Can you predict what the results of this experiment might look like, and how would you analyse & interpret the data?
  • What are the advantages/limitations of this approach?

Methods here might include:

  • screen for regulators using a reporter (e.g., beta-galactosidase) and a Tn library (will never isolate mutants in essential genes, amongst other caveats)
  • candidate-based approach (look at VirA expression in known mutant strains)
  • identify small molecules or proteins that bind VirA (how?)
